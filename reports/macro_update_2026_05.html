How the framework works, in one minute

The dashboard answers one question every Tuesday: should the portfolio be in risk assets (LONG) or sitting in cash (CASH)? It does this with a single number called MRMI.

  • MRMI above zero → LONG.
  • MRMI below zero → CASH.

What MRMI is made of

Two halves — a fast one that tracks markets, and a slow one that tracks the real economy:

MRMI = MMI + (1 − Stress) − 0.5

  • MMI — Market Momentum Index. Composite of three market signals (GII, FinCon, Breadth). Today: +0.00.
  • (1 − Stress) — the macro buffer. Stress is a 0-to-1 score that fires only when growth weakens AND inflation rises (the stagflation pocket). Today: +0.97 (Stress = 0.03 → OFF).
  • −0.5 — the threshold. Baked into the formula so MRMI's zero-line is the LONG/CASH trigger.

#### The AND-gate, today's math

Stress = min(1, max(0, −Real_Economy) × max(0, Inflation_Direction))

Today: max(0, +0.77) = 0.77 · max(0, +0.04) = 0.04 → product = 0.77 × 0.04 ≈ 0.03 → OFF

One more month of hot core CPI and stress climbs into BUILDING (0.05–0.30). That's the trigger that starts eroding the buffer.
