How the framework works, in one minute

The dashboard answers exactly one question every Tuesday: should the portfolio be in risk assets (LONG) or sitting in cash (CASH)? It does this with a single number called MRMI — the Milk Road Macro Index.

The rule is unchanged from last week:

  • MRMI above zero → LONG.
  • MRMI below zero → CASH.

What MRMI is made of

MRMI is built from two halves — a fast one that tracks markets, and a slow one that tracks the real economy. The formula is unchanged:

MRMI = MMI + (1 − Stress) − 0.5

The three pieces:

  • MMI — Market Momentum Index. A composite of three market signals (sector breadth, financial conditions, growth-impulses across credit and commodities). Today: +0.04 (last week: +0.40).
  • (1 − Stress) — the macro buffer. Today: +0.97 (last week: +1.00). Essentially intact.
  • −0.5 — the threshold. Unchanged.

We have moved from the middle of the "healthy long" band to its lower edge in one week. Not a regime change. But notable.

Why two pillars and not one

Unchanged from last week's framing — the multiplicative AND-gate inside Stress is exactly what is keeping us long today. Both axes that feed Stress are mildly adverse on their own, but neither has tipped to the side that would activate the buffer-erosion channel. The economy pillar is doing what it is designed to do: absorb a market wobble without forcing a regime flip.

#### How the AND gate combines them today

Stress = min(1, max(0, −Real_Economy) × max(0, Inflation_Direction))

Today: max(0, −(−0.79)) = 0.79 · max(0, +0.043) = 0.043 → product = 0.79 × 0.043 ≈ 0.03

Stress intensity went from 0.00 last week to 0.03 this week — practically still off. But the inflation factor is no longer clamped to zero. That is the meaningful change. Last week the AND gate was structurally disarmed because inflation was decelerating; this week it is armed and waiting for either growth weakness to deepen or inflation to accelerate further before it activates the buffer-erosion channel.

Portfolio implications

Position: stay long

Full risk-on. The framework's call is unchanged. But the call is now thin in a way it wasn't a week ago.

What the position is sensitive to

  • HY OAS widening. FinCon is the firewall. If credit reprices the fiscal story Moody's formalized, buffer erodes fast.
  • The May CPI services component. If shelter and airline fare strength persist into May data, the inflation-direction delta tips meaningfully positive while growth is already weak — that is the AND-gate trigger.
  • The Sahm indicator. Currently approaching its 0.5pp threshold. If it crosses, the Real Economy Score deteriorates further.
  • The dollar. Near 118. As long as it stays there, GII can't recover and the market pillar stays at stall.

What would trigger an action

  • MRMI moving below zero — automatic. Cash position.
  • Two consecutive weeks with MMI negative AND Stress intensity above 0.10 — the buffer is meaningfully eroding. Discretionary trim before the framework forces it.
  • HY OAS through 340bps in two prints — different credit regime. Discretionary trim regardless of MRMI.

What to watch in the next four weeks

  • May CPI release. Services / shelter / airline persistence. This is the single most important print.
  • Sahm indicator updates. Approaching threshold.
  • HY OAS and MOVE. The two credit/rates vol gauges that would signal the firewall cracking.
  • The truce. 90 days from May 14 ends August 12. Long-tail, but renegotiation timeline matters.
  • Q2 GDPNow revisions. If +4% downshifts toward +2%, Real Economy Score loses its anchor.
