How the framework works, in one minute

The dashboard answers exactly one question every Tuesday: should the portfolio be in risk assets (LONG) or sitting in cash (CASH)? It does this with a single number called MRMI — the Milk Road Macro Index.

The rule is simple:

  • MRMI above zero → LONG.
  • MRMI below zero → CASH.

That's the entire decision rule. Everything else on the dashboard is showing you why MRMI is where it is, and what might change it.

What MRMI is made of

MRMI is built from two halves — a fast one that tracks markets, and a slow one that tracks the real economy. The formula is:

```

MRMI = MMI + (1 − Stress) − 0.5

```

The three pieces:

  • MMI — Market Momentum Index. A composite of three market signals (sector breadth, financial conditions, growth-impulses across credit and commodities). It can range from roughly −3 to +3, expressed as standard deviations from its multi-year typical level. Positive = markets are constructive, negative = markets are deteriorating.
  • (1 − Stress) — the macro buffer. Stress is a 0-to-1 score capturing how deep we are inside a stagflation pocket (weak growth and rising inflation, at the same time). When stress is zero, the buffer adds a full +1 to MRMI; when stress hits 1, the buffer collapses to 0. In normal times stress sits at 0 and the buffer is intact.
  • −0.5 — the threshold. Baked into the formula so that MRMI's "go to cash" line is always exactly zero. You don't need to remember any other number.

The scale is asymmetric (more room on the upside than the downside) because the macro buffer adds a structural +0.5 bias when conditions are benign — so we live in positive territory most of the time, and downside moves typically end at the cash trigger rather than running deep.

Why two pillars and not one

Markets can flash false alarms — a single VIX spike or a credit blow-out that reverses in days. The real economy can't fake it: when growth is weakening and inflation is accelerating at the same time, that is a real condition, not noise. The framework refuses to step out of risk based on market signals alone — it requires the economy to confirm. This is why MRMI sits comfortably positive even when the market pillar (MMI) is only modestly green: as long as the economy isn't in the stagflation pocket, the buffer protects us against false triggers.

#### Why these units matter

  • z-score (3-year window). Instead of reporting raw GDP growth in dollars or PCE YoY in percent, the framework normalizes each input to "how far is this from its recent typical level, in standard deviations." −1 means clearly below typical, +1 clearly above, 0 right at average. This puts ingredients on totally different scales (PCE, jobs, income, GDPNow) onto one comparable axis.
  • pp (percentage points). When we say Core CPI YoY moved by −0.42pp, we mean the percentage itself changed by 0.42 — from say 3.0% to 2.58%. We do not mean "the rate fell by 0.42%" (which would be a 1.4% relative change, tiny). This is the standard convention in macro reporting and matters when comparing inflation prints.
  • Δ6m. "Delta over six months" — the change from the reading 6 months ago to today. Captures trajectory, not level. Inflation can be high but cooling; this number tells you which way it's moving.

#### How the AND gate combines them

```

Stress = min(1, max(0, −Real_Economy) × max(0, Inflation_Direction))

```

Each max(0, …) clamps a factor to zero when it isn't adversely positioned. Because both factors must be positive (i.e. growth weak AND inflation rising) for the product to be non-zero:

  • Today: max(0, −(−0.88)) = 0.88 · max(0, −0.42) = 0 → product = 0.88 × 0 = 0.00.

Inflation is cooling, so the gate's "inflation half" is zero, and the whole product collapses regardless of how weak growth gets. The buffer is fully intact at +1.00.

Portfolio implications

Position: stay long, full weighting

The framework's binary call is LONG. For our portfolio that means:

  • Equities: at target weight. SPX, Russell, sector ETFs — at strategic allocation. No defensive tilt.
  • Crypto: at target weight. Bitcoin alpha from MRMI has historically been smaller than the equity alpha, but the regime call is the same.
  • Cash: minimum operational level. No need to hold defensive cash above operational requirements while MRMI is positive.

What the position is sensitive to

The +0.90 reading is comfortable today but structurally lean. Two scenarios would change the call:

1. MMI rolling negative while Stress stays off (~5% probability in next 4 weeks). Breadth keeps fading; FinCon doesn't reset lower; MMI prints around −0.2. MRMI = −0.20 + 1.00 − 0.50 = +0.30. Still LONG, but barely. The buffer would be carrying the entire signal.

2. Stress flipping on while MMI holds (~10% probability in next 4 weeks). Inflation Direction crosses positive after the April CPI print (which is expected to tick up). Real Economy Score continues sliding to roughly −1.0. AND-gate fires. Stress jumps to ~0.5–0.6. Buffer compresses to +0.4. MRMI = +0.40 + 0.40 − 0.50 = +0.30. Same outcome — still LONG, but stripped of cushion.

3. Both at once (~3% probability in next 4 weeks but the asymmetric one to plan for). MMI rolls to −0.3, Stress flips on at 0.5. MRMI = −0.30 + 0.50 − 0.50 = −0.30 → CASH. This is the scenario the framework was designed to catch — and the one we should be ready to act on within the same week if it materializes.

What would trigger an action

This is the operational rule for the team:

  • MRMI prints below zero → flip portfolio to CASH at the close of the Tuesday meeting, same week. The backtest is unambiguous: delays of even one day collapse the alpha. Same-week execution captures the protection.
  • MRMI between 0 and +0.3 → meeting-by-meeting evaluation, no automatic action, but conviction is reduced.
  • MRMI above +0.3 → standard LONG operation.

What to watch in the next four weeks

Three specific data releases or signals would move the needle:

1. Today's April CPI print. Consensus expects core CPI YoY at 2.7% (up from 2.6% in March). One print won't flip the 6-month Δ from −0.42 back to positive, but a string of three or four would. Watch the trajectory, not the single print.

3. The US–China truce window. The 90-day clock started 5 May and runs until ~3 August. Both the market signal (via credit, copper, yield curve) and the economy signal (via tariff pass-through staying contained) depend on this clock. Mid-window posture shifts — extension talks, leaks, counter-tariffs — are the highest-conviction catalysts on the calendar.
