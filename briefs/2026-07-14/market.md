Growth impulses flipped positive for the first time all quarter, rising to +0.19 (7d +0.51) from -0.33 a week ago, and that single move is what kept overall market momentum at +0.59 despite pullbacks in both financial conditions and breadth. The [Baltic Dry Index rose roughly 8% over the week](https://www.handybulk.com/baltic-dry-index/) to its highest since early June, the dollar eased further to 120.50, and the yield curve held at 35 basis points with the 10-year at 4.56 and the 2-year at 4.21 — the quiet steepening underway since the June payrolls miss is now registering clearly in the growth signal, resolving what had been the most persistent weak link in the dashboard all quarter. Breadth continued its slide to +0.98 (7d -0.56), a third straight week of decline — but roughly [63% of S&P 500 stocks still trade above their 50-day averages](https://streetstats.finance/markets/breadth-momentum/SP500) and the equal-weight index has been outperforming its market-cap-weighted counterpart, which keeps the breadth pullback in the rotation-compression category rather than signaling genuine market deterioration. Financial conditions dipped to +0.59 (7d -0.19) as the VIX climbed from 15.57 to 17.16; high-yield spreads held at 2.69, so credit is not confirming the equity volatility pickup — a reassuring split, given that credit has been the more reliable stress signal throughout this cycle. Today is a [rare double-print](https://www.techtimes.com/articles/320366/20260713/wall-street-q2-bank-earnings-june-cpi-collide-tuesday-rare-double-print.htm): the June Consumer Price Index and second-quarter results from JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Bank of America, Citigroup, and Wells Fargo all landed before the open, with [consensus expecting headline inflation to fall from 4.2% to roughly 3.8% year-over-year](https://www.kiplinger.com/investing/economy/june-cpi-preview-dont-let-a-negative-headline-fool-you) on the June gasoline price decline — while core was seen holding near 2.9%, meaning the headline cooling would be driven by energy rather than any genuine underlying shift. A soft core print paired with beat-and-raise bank guidance would likely reverse the breadth slide and give financial conditions a floor; a hot core reading would start unwinding the rate-cut expectations that have done most of the work keeping this market calm since the payrolls miss, and that unwind would show up first in financial conditions and then in breadth.
