Market momentum climbed from +0.59 to +0.70 this week — its highest reading of the quarter — as the two watch-points from last week both resolved in the same direction on the same morning. The [June CPI print](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/consumer-price-index-inflation-report-june-2026.html) landed at 3.5% year-over-year with core down to 2.6% on a flat monthly reading: genuine underlying cooling, not just gasoline math, so rate-cut expectations held rather than unwound. Paired with [record bank earnings](https://cryptobriefing.com/major-banks-historic-q2-2026-earnings/) — Goldman Sachs posted its highest quarterly profit ever and JPMorgan beat consensus by a wide margin — the July 14 double-print drove wide sector participation and snapped breadth's three-week slide: breadth jumped from +0.98 to +1.35 (7d +0.07), with the share of S&P 500 stocks above their 50-day averages rising from roughly 63% to [69%](https://tickeron.com/trading-investing-101/sp-500-market-breadth-surge-69-of-stocks-above-50day-ma-signals-bullish-opportunities-for-retail-investors-in-2026/) and gains spreading across industrials, utilities, and healthcare rather than concentrating in mega-cap technology. Growth impulses were essentially unchanged at +0.18 (7d +0.24) — the [Baltic Dry continued its ascent toward 2,980](https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/baltic-dry-index-climbs-to-2797-up-50-points/) and the yield curve steepened a step further as the 2-year fell to 4.13 from 4.21 while the 10-year held at 4.55, widening the spread to about 42 basis points as the front end priced in additional cuts. Financial conditions were nearly flat at +0.58 (7d -0.25): the VIX settled back to 16.73 from 17.16 once the CPI landed, [high-yield spreads](https://convextrade.com/metrics/bamlh0a0hym2) ticked marginally wider to 2.71 but remain historically compressed, and the 7d drag captures the anxiety priced in ahead of Tuesday rather than any fresh stress that emerged after it. The watch for next week is whether breadth can hold above 1.00 as earnings season widens beyond financials — [TSMC reported 77% annual profit growth yet saw its shares fall more than 4%](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-july-14-2026) on guidance caution, underscoring that the technology bar is high, and the rotation into cyclicals and financials needs to survive a full round of sector results before the breadth improvement can be called confirmed rather than reactive.
