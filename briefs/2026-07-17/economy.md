The macro stress score crashed to +0.53 this week — a -2.73 drop — landing in Calm territory and lifting the macro buffer to +0.47, just a hair below its full-strength ceiling of +0.50. The catalyst was [Monday's June CPI report](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/14/consumer-price-index-inflation-report-june-2026.html): core inflation printed flat on the month against a consensus estimate of 0.2%, pulling the 12-month rate to 2.60% from 2.9% and flipping the six-month change in core CPI from +0.30 to +0.00 — in a single release, the inflation direction axis went from actively feeding stress into the framework to contributing nothing. Growth also quietly improved: [initial claims for the week ended July 11 came in at 208,000](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-16/us-jobless-claims-decline-to-208-000-below-economist-forecasts), a 10-week low that beat the 217,000 forecast and reversed the drift in continuing claims that last week's brief flagged as a concern, while [June retail sales rose for a fifth consecutive month](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/07/16/retail-sales-june-2026), nudging the Real Economy Score to -0.71 — up +0.12 on the week but still negative because the June payrolls shock has not left the data. The worst-case scenario from last week's brief — growth weakening and inflation re-accelerating simultaneously — did not materialize, and with both axes now pulling in a calming direction, the stress reading has de-escalated sharply. Two things would materially change the view: a July CPI showing that June's core calm was energy-driven relief masking tariff pressure building in services, or the [July 30 advance Q2 GDP report](https://www.bea.gov/news/schedule) printing well below GDPNow's current 1.3% annualized tracking, either of which could push stress back toward Watch territory.
