MMI is clinging to +0.10 — barely positive — after dropping -0.49 on the day and -0.58 over the past week, a pace of deterioration that puts the signal a small move away from going negative. The week's damage came from two directions at once: [Broadcom's quarterly results were strong in absolute terms but the company failed to raise its AI chip outlook far enough](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-june-4-2026-nasdaq-futures-slide-amid-iran-war-concerns), triggering a 9% rout in semiconductors and the Nasdaq's worst day since April 2025, which reversed the sector rotation and growth impulse signals inside GII — it fell -0.55 on the day to -0.30. Then Friday's [May payrolls print of 172,000 against an 85,000 consensus](https://247wallst.com/investing/2026/06/05/may-jobs-report-crushes-estimates-and-any-hopes-for-a-fed-rate-cut/) slammed rate-cut expectations and pushed yields higher, compounding the pressure on FinCon, which was already down -0.89 over seven days before losing another -0.52 on Friday to land at -0.09, with the VIX jumping from the mid-teens to 20.00 as equity nervousness repriced sharply. Breadth at +0.69 is the only component still clearly positive — and the sole reason MMI hasn't turned negative — but even that is eroding: it fell -0.39 on the day and -0.33 over the week, consistent with a narrowing rally where roughly [56% of S&P 500 stocks trade above their 50-day average](https://cryptodaily.co.uk/2026/06/sp500-rotation-financials-oil-yields-jobs) even as the index sits near record highs. The key threshold to watch is whether Breadth holds: if the semiconductor rout spreads rather than sparking genuine rotation into cyclicals and financials, all three MMI components go negative together and the signal flips. What would stabilize the read is chips finding a floor, credit spreads staying tight (no material blow-out yet), and the strong jobs data getting re-read as an earnings positive rather than purely a Fed headwind.
