The stress score sits at +3.53, firmly in Watch territory and up +1.52 over the past seven days, after June's payrolls report resolved the prior brief's central test with a clear miss. The [Bureau of Labor Statistics reported 57,000 jobs added in June](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm) — barely half the 100,000 consensus — while downward revisions to April and May wiped out a combined 74,000 more jobs than first reported, confirming the hiring slowdown was already entrenched before last month's count. The Real Economy Score is -0.90 (7d -0.41), barely off its prior low and still deeply negative: the unemployment rate edged down to 4.2%, but only because [labor force participation fell to 61.5%, its lowest since March 2021](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/02/jobs-report-june-2026-.html), with prime-age workers exiting at the fastest monthly pace outside the pandemic in over a decade — fewer people searching is not the same as more people working. The inflation axis offers no new pressure and no new relief: Inflation Direction holds at +0.30 (7d +0.00), core CPI sits at 2.90%, and June's wage growth of 0.3% month-over-month is not adding upward fuel. The macro buffer is at +0.32, still a positive tailwind to the headline allocation posture, but more than a third eroded from its full-strength level of +0.50, with limited runway left before further growth deterioration pushes it toward zero. The most immediate tests are [ISM Services on July 6](https://www.ismworld.org/supply-management-news-and-reports/reports/rob-report-calendar/) and a GDPNow update on July 7 — neither has yet absorbed the payrolls miss — followed by the [July 14 core CPI print](https://www.bls.gov/schedule/news_release/cpi.htm); if inflation begins to lift while growth stays soft, both axes would be pulling in the same direction, which is where the framework registers its steepest stress increases.
