Market momentum barely moved on the surface — +0.69 today versus +0.65 at the last read — but the composition underneath shifted significantly, and the previous brief's watch point came true in form but not in consequence. Tech did crack: semiconductors and large artificial intelligence hardware stocks led sharp losses through the holiday-shortened week, and breadth retreated from +1.95 to +1.76 (7d -0.42). But the retreat reflects rotation more than risk-off — [the S&P 500 advance-decline line hit fresh all-time highs](https://streetstats.finance/markets/breadth-momentum/SP500) as industrials, financials, and retail filled the gap, with the equal-weight index making new highs even as the cap-weighted index is dragged lower by the largest technology names; at +1.76, breadth is still well into positive territory, and the sector spread of the gains means the rally is not a single-theme trade. Financial conditions are now the component doing the most work, jumping from +0.41 to +0.70 (+0.30 on the week): [all 32 major U.S. banks cleared the Federal Reserve's stress tests](https://www.fool.com/investing/2026/07/01/the-feds-bank-stress-test-results-are-in-heres-wha/), [JPMorgan announced a $50 billion buyback with a 10% dividend increase](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/24/jpmorgan-goldman-sachs-fed-stress-test.html), Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley followed with similar capital return programs, and the resulting confidence in the banking system compressed high-yield spreads from 2.83 to 2.74 and pulled VIX down from 17.65 to 16.15 — credit and equity markets moving in the same direction, which is exactly what the financial conditions component is built to reward. Growth impulses remain the structural anchor at -0.40 (7d +0.10): the yield curve is still only 31 basis points wide between the 2-year and 10-year, the dollar is unchanged at 120.89, and the seven-day drift is barely positive — this component has been negative all quarter and is not close to flipping. The buffer is thinner than +0.69 implies because financial conditions — now the dominant prop — are fragile to the same risk that bent them in June: [futures markets are pricing a Federal Reserve hike before year-end](https://streetstats.finance/rates/fedfunds) with core inflation still above 3%, and earnings season kicks off mid-July alongside another inflation print; one hawkish data surprise could quickly re-widen credit spreads and reverse most of what the bank capital story just built.
