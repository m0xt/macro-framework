The macro stress pressure score is sitting at its historical zero-point — z-score essentially flat — signaling that the AND-condition between weak growth and rising inflation remains far from threatening, with the hard-clipped stress intensity at zero and the macro buffer feeding MRMI at full strength. The two underlying axes are each mildly adverse but not simultaneously enough to build pressure: the real economy score has slipped to roughly −0.9σ, consistent with a labor market the [April payrolls report](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/08/jobs-report-april-2026.html) described as effectively "frozen" — 115,000 net adds well below trend, the unemployment rate unchanged at 4.3%, and wage growth at 3.6% annualized, shy of estimates — though the Atlanta Fed's [Q2 GDPNow estimate of 3.7%](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow) is anchoring the growth sub-component and preventing a deeper slide into the danger zone. On the inflation axis, the six-month directional change in core CPI remains negative at roughly −0.4pp, meaning that despite today's [April CPI release](https://www.marketplace.org/story/2026/05/11/economists-expect-april-cpi-to-show-rising-inflation) arriving with consensus expecting a tick up to 2.7% core YoY (from March's 2.6%), the trend that actually feeds the model — the six-month drift — has not reversed; energy spillovers from the Middle East conflict are inflating the headline print but have not yet meaningfully re-accelerated core. The AND-gate multiplicative structure means these two modestly adverse readings, when multiplied together, produce near-zero intensity — neither axis is sufficiently extreme to overwhelm the other. What would shift this materially: a confirmed pivot in the six-month core CPI direction into positive territory over the next two to three months — especially if the [tariff pass-through Goldman estimates at roughly 1 percentage point of incremental inflation](https://www.morningstar.com/economy/inflation-set-rise-tariff-costs-hit-consumers-2026) accelerates — running concurrently with the real economy score deepening below −1σ on continued labor market softening. Until those two axes turn adverse in tandem, the pressure z-score stays near norm and the buffer remains intact.
