The defining tension in this week's MMI read is a sharp internal divergence: GII is the lone driver gaining ground on a seven-day basis (+0.165), while Breadth has given back more than half a point (-0.655) and FinCon has softened modestly, leaving the composite at +0.394 — still green, but on a clearly deteriorating weekly trajectory. The GII lift traces directly to Monday's [US-China 90-day tariff truce](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-china-tariffs-deal-90-days-trump-admin-trade-talks-progress/) struck in Geneva: copper [surged to $10,296/tonne](https://carboncredits.com/copper-prices-surge-to-10296-tonne-as-us-china-truce-sparks-market-rally/) on the announcement, HY spreads compressed further toward multi-year tights, and the yield curve — now 48 bps positively sloped at the 10s-2s — reinforced the forward-risk bid across GII's components. Breadth's deterioration tells the other side of the story: the [S&P 500 ripped roughly 14% in 23 sessions](https://www.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/small-caps-steal-the-spotlight-as-sp-500-rally-masks-weak-breadth-4654838) since the early-April lows, but fewer than 60% of its constituents were trading above their 200-day moving averages at the highs — a participation deficit our cyclical ETF basket (semis, transports, homebuilders, regional banks, retail) captured as those sectors cooled sharply after their tariff-relief spike. FinCon remains modestly positive but lost ground intraday as VIX at 18.90 still carries enough residual fear premium to drag the component; MOVE at 70.74 is constructively low, however, signaling that bond vol has disengaged from equity anxiety — a split that often precedes equity vol normalization. The net read: GII is providing real fundamental support from the trade détente, but MMI cannot sustain a move higher unless Breadth closes the gap — the rally is genuine, yet still narrow enough relative to its headline that cyclical sector participation, not the truce itself, is the variable to watch in the sessions ahead.
