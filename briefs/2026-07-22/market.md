Market momentum has cleared the edge it was teetering on yesterday, recovering to +0.44 (1d +0.20) from +0.24 and moving comfortably back into LONG territory. Financial conditions drove the recovery, jumping from +0.33 to +0.54 (1d +0.21): the [VIX fell to 17.05 from 18.65](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-07-21/stock-market-today-dow-s-p-live-updates) as the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index surged 5% and the broader tech sector added 2.4% on Tuesday, with high-yield credit spreads narrowing a further notch to 2.69 — confirming once again that the AI equity volatility is contained to stocks and has not spread into debt markets. Breadth has partly followed, recovering to +0.89 (1d +0.16) from +0.73, but remains below the 1.00 line: [the rally stayed chip-led](https://cryptodaily.co.uk/2026/07/sp500-sector-breadth-rebound-earnings-proof), with participation from industrials, transports, and other cyclicals still thin enough that the breadth score reflects a partial repair rather than genuine broadening of market support. Growth impulses posted their best single day of the past week, bouncing to -0.10 (1d +0.24), yet remain the only negative component in the index: the [Baltic Dry Index slid to 2,671 on Monday](https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/baltic-dry-index-falls-to-2671-down-81-points/) and has not recovered, and sector rotation has still not found a new cyclical destination to replace the capital that left semiconductors earlier in the month. The week-over-week readings put today's move in context — market momentum (7d -0.23) and growth impulses (7d -0.44) are well below where they stood seven days ago, so this is a dip-recovery rather than the start of a new positive leg. [Alphabet reports after the close tonight](https://eciks.org/14723-28767-alphabet-q2-earnings-july-22-ai-spending), and its guidance feeds directly into the two weakest parts of the framework: strong Google Cloud growth and firm AI spending commitments would push growth impulses through zero and begin unwinding that seven-day drag, while any softness in forward demand would likely reverse today's financial conditions recovery and push market momentum back toward the LONG/CAUTION boundary it just escaped.
