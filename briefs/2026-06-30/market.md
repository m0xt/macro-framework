Market momentum rose to +0.65 this week — up +0.19 over seven days from last week's +0.47 — as the equity rally gained its widest participation in recent weeks: breadth surged to +1.95 (+0.55 on the week), with gains spreading well beyond the technology names that had led since the ceasefire. Last week's brief flagged a specific risk — that a [PCE print above the Fed's own freshly-raised 3.6% forecast](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/25/pce-inflation-report-may-2026-.html) would simultaneously hit volatility and crack the financial conditions reading holding everything together. [PCE did print hot at 4.1% headline and 3.4% core](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/pce-inflation-reached-highest-level-174530224.html), the highest readings in three years, but markets absorbed it without a volatility event: VIX fell from 19.62 to 17.65 and MOVE ticked up only slightly to 66.79. Financial conditions weakened anyway, down -0.22 on the week to +0.41, as high yield spreads drifted wider to 2.83 from 2.66 — the [credit market beginning, very tentatively, to reprice a Fed that has now explicitly signaled a hike before year-end](https://ferrantecapitaladvisers.com/insights/high-yield-credit-spreads-cycle-low-2026-default-divergence/). Growth impulses improved from -0.62 to -0.40 (+0.22 on the week), aided partly by a fractionally wider yield curve at 31 basis points between the 2-year and 10-year, though the structural drags — a still-flat curve and a dollar at 120.89 — have not cleared, leaving growth impulses negative and the macro divergence only partially resolved. Breadth at +1.95 is the headline reason the dashboard reads as well as it does: that kind of wide participation means the rally is not a narrow bet on a few names, and it gives market momentum enough buffer to absorb continued softening in financial conditions. The watch going forward is whether [mid-week weakness in technology stocks](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-26-2026) — driven by rising artificial intelligence infrastructure cost concerns and a reported delay to the OpenAI public offering — spreads to broader sectors and drags breadth back, because if it does, the main buffer holding the current market momentum score comes off the board.
