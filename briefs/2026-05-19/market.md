The MMI is clinging to flat (-0.003) after a violent seven-day unwind (-0.402 on the week), and the damage is almost entirely a bond market story — FinCon is the heaviest drag (-0.093 on the session, -0.379 on the week), with the [MOVE index sitting at 86](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/VIXCLS) and rate vol overwhelming any comfort VIX's 18 handle might otherwise provide. The proximate catalyst was [Moody's stripping the U.S. of its final Aaa rating on May 16](https://ratings.moodys.com/ratings-news/443154), pushing the 10-year to a one-year high near 4.59% and briefly sending 30-year yields through 5% — a signal that the term premium demanded of sovereign paper is durably repricing, not just spiking. A [Fortune report on May 17](https://fortune.com/2026/05/17/us-debt-japan-investors-treasury-bonds-top-foreign-holders-repatriation-jgb-treasury-yields/) deepened the anxiety: Japanese institutions, now facing their own 10-year JGB yields at a 29-year high near 2.8% and BOJ tightening expectations, are the marginal buyer most likely to slow or reverse their Treasury participation — amplifying whatever demand shortfall the downgrade catalyzed. Breadth took the second-biggest hit (-0.605 over seven days), as the violent repricing of long-duration assets rotated capital out of rate-sensitive cyclicals that had been driving participation, collapsing the sector breadth signal to effectively flat (-0.017) from a far stronger position earlier in the month. The one holdout is GII, which remains in positive territory (+0.100) because copper's structural bid — sustained by [AI data-center buildout demand and a J.P. Morgan-projected 330,000-ton refined shortfall](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/commodities/copper-outlook) — and a [still-compressed HY OAS near 280bp](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2) are keeping the real-economy growth-impulse signals from rolling over. The internal divergence between a positive GII and negative Breadth/FinCon is the key tension: credit markets and commodities are not yet pricing a growth accident, but the sovereign fiscal premium is tightening financial conditions through rate vol in a way that equities and cyclical participation are now feeling.
