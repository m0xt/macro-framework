The macro stress pressure score has climbed sharply this week — the raw reading reached +0.430 against a neutral baseline of ~0.25, a +0.172 move in seven days that pushes the z-score toward the +1σ threshold where stress begins running above the historical norm. The catalyst was unambiguous: [April's CPI print](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/cpi-inflation-april-2026-.html) released May 12 showed core CPI jumping to 2.8% year-over-year with a 0.4% monthly gain — the hottest monthly reading since January 2025 — which snapped the inflation direction axis from near-flat to positive, and that axis's +0.461 pp seven-day move is the mechanical footprint of the tariff channel beginning to pass through as pre-tariff import inventories run down. Growth is preventing the pressure score from moving higher still: the real economy score at −0.775 is meaningfully below zero, reflecting softening across PCE, the Sahm framework, and real incomes (real average wages fell 0.3% year-over-year per the same CPI release), but GDPNow's positive z-score and [three consecutive months of retail sales gains](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/cpi-inflation-april-2026-.html) suggest the economy is decelerating rather than contracting — the +0.106 seven-day improvement in the real economy score corroborates that the trajectory has at least stabilized. The divergence that matters is the vector: growth is weakening gradually while inflation is re-accelerating quickly, which is precisely the AND-condition the stress framework is built to penalize. [Moody's stripping the U.S. sovereign Aaa rating on May 16](https://ratings.moodys.com/ratings-news/443154) — the last of the three major agencies to do so — added structural texture to this backdrop, driving the 30-year Treasury yield above 5% and widening the fiscal discount rate precisely when consumer balance sheets are already under strain from delinquency rates approaching post-GFC highs. The hard-clipped stress intensity remains near zero (+0.033) and the MRMI macro buffer is essentially intact at 0.967, but the pressure z-score will cross into genuine +1σ territory and begin threatening the buffer if the May core CPI print sustains a 0.3–0.4% monthly pace, or if labor market data — particularly a Sahm-rule deterioration — pushes the real economy score meaningfully more negative from here.
