Macro stress sits at **0.03 — OFF**, but the inputs that feed it have shifted materially this week. The headline reading is benign because the framework only activates stress when *both* growth weakens AND inflation accelerates simultaneously, and only one of those two has moved hard. The catalyst was unambiguous: [April's CPI print](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/cpi-inflation-april-2026-.html) released May 12 showed core CPI jumping to 2.8% year-over-year with a 0.4% monthly gain — the hottest monthly reading since January 2025 — flipping the inflation input from cooling to accelerating. That move is the mechanical footprint of the tariff channel beginning to pass through as pre-tariff import inventories run down.

Growth is what's keeping the bucket OFF. The growth input is still meaningfully weak — softening across PCE, the Sahm framework, and real incomes (real average wages fell 0.3% year-over-year per the same CPI release) — but it's actually *improved slightly* this week, not deteriorated, with GDPNow positive and [three consecutive months of retail sales gains](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/cpi-inflation-april-2026-.html) suggesting the economy is decelerating rather than contracting.

The vector that matters: growth is weakening *gradually* while inflation has re-accelerated *quickly*. That asymmetry is what the AND-gate stress framework is built to catch — a stagflation pocket where both axes turn adverse together. Today we are not yet in that pocket, but the inflation move is the kind of shift that, if it continues, pulls stress out of OFF and into BUILDING (the 0.05–0.30 band) within one or two more weekly prints.

[Moody's stripping the U.S. sovereign Aaa rating on May 16](https://ratings.moodys.com/ratings-news/443154) — the last of the three major agencies to do so — added structural texture: the 30-year Treasury yield pushed above 5%, widening the fiscal discount rate precisely when consumer balance sheets are already strained by delinquency rates near post-GFC highs.

What would move stress into BUILDING from here: a May core CPI print sustaining a 0.3–0.4% monthly pace (inflation input pushes harder positive), or labor-market data — particularly a Sahm-rule deterioration — pushing the growth input meaningfully weaker. Either alone tips the AND-gate product into the BUILDING band; both together is the genuine ELEVATED scenario.
