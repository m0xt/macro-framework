MMI comes into May 27 at +0.473, a healthy positive reading built almost entirely on exceptional breadth and steadily improving financial conditions, with growth impulses barely pulling their weight. Breadth at 0.903 is near the top of its range: [Tuesday's fresh S&P 500 and Nasdaq record closes](https://www.fool.com/coverage/stock-market-today/2026/05/26/stock-market-today-may-26-soaring-tech-stocks-push-s-and-p-500-and-nasdaq-to-new-highs/) showed tech, semiconductors, and mega-caps all moving together — [Micron alone surged 19%](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-may-26-2026) on AI demand while AMD and Alphabet kept pace — which is the kind of broad participation, not narrow leadership, that gives the breadth score conviction. Financial conditions are calm: VIX held below 18, MOVE (bond vol) sits at 75, and [high-yield spreads near 274 basis points](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/bofa-merrill-lynch-us-high-yield-option-adjusted-spread-fed-data.html) are historically compressed, meaning credit markets are pricing almost no stress. The growth impulse is the holdout at just +0.062, and its sharp single-day drop (-0.258) tells the story: [copper retreated from its mid-May highs](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/copper-industrial-metals-inflation-bond-yields.html) as industrial demand signals turned mixed, [freight networks remain disrupted by Middle East vessel rerouting](https://www.chrobinson.com/en-us/resources/insights-and-advisories/north-america-freight-insights/may-2026-freight-market-update/), and the [Moody's Aa1 downgrade of US sovereign debt](https://ratings.moodys.com/ratings-news/443154) from two weeks ago has kept 10-year yields sticky near 4.56%, which dulls the yield-curve contribution the model wants to see. The week's strong MMI surge (+0.336 over seven days) traces largely to [US-Iran peace optimism](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/25/stock-futures-today-live-updates.html) that cut oil prices, eased near-term inflation fears, and broadly lifted risk appetite — a geopolitical driver that can reverse just as fast. What would change the read: breadth rarely stays above 0.90 without underlying earnings and economic support catching up, so if growth impulses stay flat and sector rotation stalls, the two-legged stool wobbles; separately, a 10-year yield move decisively above 4.65% on fiscal concerns — the congressional deficit debate is still live — would pressure financial conditions and is the cleaner single variable to watch for a regime shift.
