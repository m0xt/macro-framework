The stress score barely moved this week, settling at +1.91 (+0.05) and holding comfortably in the Calm band while the macro buffer continued its slow drift to +0.40 — down from +0.41 last week and +0.44 the week before, but still providing near-full support to the MRMI posture. The previous brief flagged the FOMC as the key swing factor, and the [June 17 meeting](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) delivered the most hawkish signal this cycle: the [dot plot flipped to project a hike before year-end](https://www.stocktitan.net/articles/fed-rate-decision-june-17-2026), 9 of 18 officials are now penciling in at least one 25bp increase, and 17 of 18 flagged upside inflation risk — Warsh himself notably abstained from the dot plot entirely, making the committee's actual lean harder to pin down. The dashboard's stress score barely reacted, though, because this framework runs on realized data rather than forward projections: the Inflation Direction axis held exactly at +0.30 (unchanged on the week), core CPI YoY stayed at 2.90%, and the [Real Economy Score](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow) barely moved at -0.47 (-0.01). [GDPNow ticked back up toward 3.0% annualized for Q2](https://www.fx.co/en/forex-news/3033881) and [initial jobless claims eased to 226,000](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/articles/u-weekly-jobless-claims-fall-135748098.html), so the real economy is below trend but stable — not the kind of deterioration that would push stress higher on its own. The risk is that the two axes are now pulling in opposite directions in slow motion: growth is soft but not cracking, while inflation is no longer cooling and the Fed has effectively declared the next move is more likely up than down. What changes the dashboard view is the [June CPI print on July 14](https://www.bls.gov/schedule/news_release/cpi.htm): another month of sticky or accelerating core goods — the tariff pass-through the previous brief flagged — would push the +0.30 Inflation Direction reading higher and start moving the stress score toward the Watch band in earnest, at which point the macro buffer erosion would accelerate and the MRMI tailwind would begin to fade.
