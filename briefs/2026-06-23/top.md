The hawkish dot plot answered last week's central question: rather than consolidating at the LONG threshold, [the FOMC's projected hike](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260617a.htm) — nine officials now seeing at least one increase this year, the median year-end rate at 3.8% — pulled MRMI from +0.25 back to +0.12, a clear CAUTION at 75% exposure. Both pillars absorbed the shock without breaking: on the market side, MMI fell to +0.47 from +0.59 as GII dropped to -0.62 (the yield curve compressed to 27 basis points, DXY pushed to 120.40), but HY credit spreads held at 2.66 and MOVE fell to 65.39, so the damage was in rate structure rather than financial conditions broadly — the repricing the previous brief flagged landed in the curve, not in credit or volatility. The economy side barely moved — macro buffer at +0.40, stress at +1.91, both still firmly Calm — and [May retail sales at +0.9% month-over-month](https://www.census.gov/retail/sales.html) pushed GDPNow back to 3.0%, showing the consumer absorbing the rate shift better than the bond market is pricing, though the Real Economy Score at -0.47 reflects that one strong print doesn't erase the softer jobs composition from earlier in the quarter. What's holding the framework together is breadth at +1.40: the broad sector participation from the ceasefire rally has barely eroded, and as long as it holds, MMI retains enough margin above +0.25 to stay LONG even with GII underwater. With [markets now pricing a potential October hike](https://www.cmegroup.com/markets/interest-rates/cme-fedwatch-tool.html), inflation direction at +0.30 — unchanged for two weeks — is the single hinge for both pillars: the [July 14 CPI release](https://www.bls.gov/cpi/) either validates the Fed's 3.6% PCE projection and begins eroding the macro buffer (a second consecutive +0.3% core print pushes MRMI deeper into CAUTION), or supports the tariff-pass-through-is-done thesis and gives growth room to pull the index back toward LONG. The posture is stable CAUTION — 75% exposure with markets intact but the rate-regime overhang capping further gains until inflation gives a clearer answer.
