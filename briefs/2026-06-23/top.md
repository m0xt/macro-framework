MRMI pulled back from +0.25 to +0.12 this week — the FOMC resolved last week's central question exactly as the downside scenario described: rather than the balanced hold that would have let the financial conditions recovery consolidate and tip MRMI into RISK-ON, Warsh delivered a dot plot that flipped to signal a hike, the 2-year yield surged more than 16 basis points on the day, and the growth impulse index (GII) fell another -0.31 to -0.62, dragging MMI down -0.12 to +0.47. What's notable is how well the market absorbed the shock on the other dimensions: breadth held at +1.40 and financial conditions barely moved at +0.62, confirming the broad post-ceasefire participation is sticky and preventing a deeper MMI decline — the market is repricing rate expectations without broadly selling equities. The economy pillar is telling a nearly identical story from its own data: stress edged up only +0.05 to +1.91, the macro buffer drifted to +0.40, GDPNow held near 3.0%, and claims eased to 226,000 — growth is below trend but not breaking, and the inflation direction axis stayed exactly at +0.30. Both pillars are now aligned around the same unresolved tension: the real economy is soft enough that the expansion looks unconvincing, inflation is no longer cooling, and the Fed has declared its next move is more likely a hike than a cut — yet nothing in the realized data has cracked sharply enough to push stress higher or drag breadth down. That tension gets its first hard test Thursday with the [May PCE release](https://fxmacrodata.com/articles/usd-pce-may-2026): April headline PCE was already at 3.80% and core at 3.30%, well above target, and a May print at or above the Fed's freshly-raised 3.6% forecast would push the inflation direction reading above +0.30, accelerate macro buffer erosion, and give GII's skeptical growth read harder backing — the combination that makes a sustained move to RISK-ON genuinely difficult. A softer number would do the opposite: validate breadth and FinCon's resilience, give GII room to stabilize, and put MRMI back within reach of +0.25 threshold.
