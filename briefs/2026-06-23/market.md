MMI pulled back to +0.47 this week, down -0.12 from last week's +0.59, as the [FOMC June 17](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html) answered the open question from last week's brief — and answered it at the hawkish end. Chair Warsh held rates at 3.50–3.75% but the dot plot [flipped from implying cuts to implying a hike](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/warsh-hawkish-shock-9-fed-180221394.html): nine of eighteen officials now project at least one increase before year-end, the median end-2026 rate moved to 3.8%, and the PCE inflation forecast jumped from 2.7% to 3.6%. The 2-year yield [surged more than 16 basis points on the day](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/18/treasury-yields-investors-warsh-fed-interest-rates.html) — the biggest single-day move on an FOMC meeting date since March 2008 — and the S&P 500 fell roughly 1.2%. Despite that shock, FinCon held flat on the week at +0.62 (-0.00 over 7d), because MOVE dropped from 69.36 to 65.39, absorbing the VIX rise from 17.68 to 19.62: the bond market had apparently already priced a good chunk of Warsh's hawkishness before Tuesday. Breadth slipped only slightly to +1.40 (-0.06 over 7d), suggesting the broad post-ceasefire participation is sticky — the market didn't sell off across the board, just reset. The real problem remains GII at -0.62 (-0.31 over 7d): the growth impulse signals — credit, [copper stalling near record highs](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/copper-industrial-metals-inflation-bond-yields.html) rather than accelerating, a nearly flat yield curve (27 basis points between 2s and 10s), and a ripping dollar at DXY 120.40 — keep signaling that the underlying macro momentum has not confirmed the equity optimism. HY credit spreads at 2.66 are still very tight and give GII its floor, but the divergence between tight credit and soft growth impulses is the dashboard's unresolved tension. The view holds LONG for now, but another week of GII deterioration without a breadth or FinCon offset would start to close the gap to the CAUTION threshold; the next hard test is PCE later this week, which if it prints above the Fed's own freshly-raised forecast, would hit VIX and MOVE simultaneously and stress the FinCon score that's been holding this whole picture together.
