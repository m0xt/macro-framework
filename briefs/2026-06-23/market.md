The FOMC risk flagged in last week's brief materialized in full: [Warsh's June 17 debut](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html) brought the exact hawkish dot-plot shift that was feared — nine of eighteen officials now lean toward a 2026 hike, the [median year-end funds rate was revised up to 3.8%](https://www.tradingkey.com/analysis/economic/central-banks/261973912-fed-federal-fomc-2-economic-projections-decision-rates-tradingkey), and the PCE inflation forecast jumped nearly a full point — yet MMI barely moved, slipping to +0.55 (7d -0.04). The reason is FinCon, which rose (7d +0.20) to +0.83 in spite of the hawkish reset: MOVE fell from 69.36 to 65.39, meaning bond markets absorbed the policy shift without repricing near-term uncertainty higher, while VIX edged down to 17.28 and HY credit spreads stayed tight at 2.66. Equities helped their own case — a Juneteenth-shortened week ended with stocks recovering nearly all of Wednesday's ~1% FOMC selloff, the Nasdaq up 2.4% for the week, with confirmation of the Iran peace agreement locking in the Strait of Hormuz reopening and late-week tech strength adding fuel. Breadth remains strong at +1.40 (7d -0.06) but showed early signs of narrowing: only five of eleven S&P sectors closed higher on Friday and Nasdaq breadth eased, which is normal cooling after a broad relief rally but worth tracking if it continues next week. The persistent weak link is GII, which deepened to -0.57 (7d -0.25) — growth impulse signals across credit, rotation, yield curve, and shipping still aren't corroborating what equity prices are doing; copper near record highs looks like genuine demand strength on the surface but is largely a [tariff-front-running distortion](https://www.fxstreet.com/analysis/whats-next-for-us-copper-import-tariffs-202606121140), with the U.S. sitting on record stockpiles while the rest of the world faces a shortage, and a Commerce Secretary tariff recommendation to Trump is [due by June 30](https://www.fxstreet.com/analysis/whats-next-for-us-copper-import-tariffs-202606121140) — making it a live input to GII in the next reading. MMI holds the LONG zone comfortably, but the gap between a +1.40 Breadth and a -0.57 GII is the starkest internal tension in the dashboard: either growth impulse catches up and confirms the rally, or breadth eventually follows GII lower — and any renewed Warsh hawkishness that pushes MOVE back up would be the fastest path to the second outcome.
