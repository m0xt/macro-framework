The hawkish FOMC the previous brief flagged as the week's key risk has landed, and MMI absorbed it without breaking — pulling back to +0.47 from +0.59, a -0.12 decline on the week, but staying firmly LONG. [The dot plot was the shock](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/17/fed-interest-rate-decision-june-2026.html): nine of 18 Fed officials now project at least one rate hike before year-end, lifting the median policy rate endpoint from 3.4% to 3.8%, and the [S&P 500 fell 1.21%](https://www.thestreet.com/stock-market-today/stock-market-today-dow-jones-sp-500-nasdaq-updates-june-17-2026) on the day while the 2-year yield jumped more than 16 basis points. The brief's biggest miss was on bond volatility: rather than blowing out as feared, MOVE fell to 65.39 from 69.36 — the hold itself was confirmed and [Warsh's hawkish lean](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/articles/warsh-hawkish-shock-9-fed-180221394.html) was already partly in the price — and with HY credit spreads staying historically tight at 2.66, FinCon ended the week essentially flat at +0.62. Equity vol told a different story: VIX recovered from its post-Fed spike toward the low-16s by Thursday, then crept back to 19.62 as [lingering uncertainty about the Iran deal timeline and Strait of Hormuz access](https://www.home.saxo/content/articles/macro/market-quick-take---19-june-2026-19062026) kept markets unsettled through the back half of the week. The real damage landed in GII, which dropped to -0.62 from -0.31: the hawkish shift compressed the yield curve further — the 10Y/2Y gap is now just 27 basis points with the 10Y at 4.46% and the 2Y at 4.19% — and a strong dollar at DXY 120.40 adds another headwind to the credit, copper, and rotation signals GII tracks. Breadth at +1.40 is the load-bearing element: broad market participation from the ceasefire rally has barely eroded, and it's the main reason MMI sits nearly 20 basis points above the +0.25 line separating LONG from CAUTION. The framework holds LONG for now, but the condition is GII stabilizing — if the yield curve compresses further or credit begins to widen on repriced rate expectations, MMI's margin above +0.25 gets thin fast.
