The stress score holds at +0.53 — Calm — with the macro buffer at +0.47, near the full-strength ceiling, where both settled after last week's CPI relief. The most striking new data point this week is the [Philadelphia Fed Manufacturing Index jumping to 41.4 in July from 10.3 in June](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/07/16/philadelphia-fed-manufacturing-index-june-2026), far above the 13.0 consensus and the highest reading since November 2021, a signal that manufacturing demand is recovering at a pace the tariff headlines hadn't predicted. [Housing starts beat estimates by 9% at 1.427 million annualized](https://www.census.gov/construction/nrc/pdf/newresconst.pdf) and [University of Michigan consumer sentiment rose to a five-month high at 54.4](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/07/17/consumer-sentiment-hits-highest-level-since-february-on-easing-gas-prices), with all five components improving broadly — though most responses were collected before the mid-July uptick in gasoline prices after resumed US strikes on Iran, which means the improvement is probably slightly overstated. Taken together, these prints held [GDPNow's Q2 tracking at 1.7%](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow) as of July 17, but the Real Economy Score is still -0.70 because June's payroll shortfall keeps the labor component below zero and the hard-data recovery has not fully closed that gap. The inflation read is mixed: Michigan one-year inflation expectations eased to 4.2% from 4.6%, consistent with the +0.00 direction reading, but the Philly Fed's input-cost gauge — what manufacturers say they are paying for materials — came in at 53.9, confirming that tariff costs are still flowing through the manufacturing pipeline and that the pass-through risk flagged last week has not resolved. The framework stays Calm unless inflation direction flips positive or growth deteriorates materially; Q2 GDP on July 30 will test whether the labor gap is closing, and the July CPI on August 12 remains the cleaner of the two triggers — services re-acceleration there would push inflation direction positive and rebuild stress quickly.
