The macro stress score sits at **0.815** — squarely in Calm territory — and with the buffer feeding MRMI nearly intact at 0.459 out of a possible 0.50, the economy pillar is doing close to zero damage to overall allocation posture right now. The real economy score is the only axis adding any weight, printing at -0.672 z-score, which signals that the equal-weighted composite of PCE, Sahm, real income, and GDPNow is below its historical median — but notably it nudged upward by +0.10 over the past week, the direction that matters. That modest recovery is being driven largely by the [Atlanta Fed GDPNow tracking Q2 at 4.3%](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow) as of May 21 — up from 4.0% the prior week — after Census and Fed data revised both real PCE growth (to 2.9%) and gross private domestic investment (to 11.4%) higher; AI-driven capex is carrying a disproportionate share of the growth load while consumer spending tracks closer to 1.6%, the softer component keeping the real economy z below zero. On the inflation axis, the 6-month delta in core CPI is barely positive at +0.043 pp — meaning the framework sees essentially no acceleration pressure — even though [April's headline CPI printed 3.8% YoY and core came in at +0.4% MoM](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/12/cpi-inflation-april-2026-.html), the hottest monthly core reading since January 2025; the 6-month smoothing absorbs single-month jumps, and the critical detail from the April report is that [tariff-sensitive goods categories — apparel, new vehicles, household furnishings — all fell or decelerated](https://verifiedinvesting.com/blogs/us-economic-metrics/us-cpi-april-2026-core-doubles-services-not-tariffs), with services driving the beat instead, consistent with [Dallas Fed research showing tariff pass-through to PCE prices likely peaked in Q1](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2026/0505-mau). The primary risk that could push stress from Calm toward Watch is the April PCE release due May 28: if core PCE follows the CPI's services acceleration, the 6-month inflation delta begins climbing in earnest, and any coincident softening in real income or consumer spending would hit both axes simultaneously — which is where the stress score builds fastest.
