MMI ended the week at **+0.366**, a sharp recovery from where it was seven days ago (+0.479 on the week), and all three of its sub-components are sitting in positive territory — breadth is the strongest leg, followed by financial conditions, then the growth impulse. The big headline was [Moody's stripping the US of its last Aaa sovereign rating](https://ratings.moodys.com/ratings-news/443154) on the Friday before Memorial Day weekend; it landed with an initial thud — stocks dipped more than 1% Monday morning and the 30-year Treasury briefly crossed 5% — but [markets absorbed the shock within hours](https://www.nbcnews.com/business/markets/markets-closing-numbers-monday-moodys-us-credit-downgrade-rcna207730), and neither credit nor volatility broke. That resilience shows up directly in the financial conditions sub-component (+0.299): HY credit spreads at [278 basis points](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2) are near multi-decade tights, the VIX closed at 16.74, and the MOVE — the bond-market equivalent of the VIX — sits at a relatively calm 78, all of which means borrowing conditions remain genuinely easy and stress is contained. Breadth (+0.599) is the most encouraging piece: [the rally has broadened well beyond mega-cap tech](https://cryptodaily.co.uk/2026/05/sp500-breadth-beyond-big-tech), with small caps and equal-weight indices outrunning the cap-weighted index, which is exactly the kind of broad participation that sustains momentum rather than just inflating a few big names. The growth impulse sub-component is the softer leg at +0.201 — copper and sector rotation support it, but the 10-year yield sitting at 4.57% reflects genuine fiscal anxiety as Congress advances a [large deficit-widening bill](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/05/22/treasury-yields-snapshot-may-22-2026), and a DXY above 119 adds mild drag for risk assets globally. What would flip the read: a meaningful move wider in HY spreads — even from historically tight levels a 30–40bp gap-out would erode FinCon fast — or a VIX/MOVE spike if bond markets decide the fiscal trajectory requires a real premium rather than just a headline worry.
