MRMI prints at +0.074 — above the CASH floor but a meaningful 18 points short of the +0.25 line that would flip posture to LONG — and the gap is not a disagreement between pillars so much as a recognition that strong momentum and subdued stress together still don't resolve the fundamental ambiguity sitting inside both stories. The market pillar is delivering the more forceful read: MMI at +0.365 reflects genuine broad-based risk appetite, with credit spreads near multi-year tights, VIX at 16.81, and cyclical sectors confirming what mega-cap tech started, all catalyzed by [geopolitical de-escalation on the US-Iran track](https://blog.kraken.com/economic-brief/may-20-2026) that has compressed the risk premium across equities, credit, and rate vol simultaneously. The economy pillar is nearly as clean on its face — stress at 0.815 and a macro buffer of +0.459 out of a possible 0.50 means the framework is extracting almost no penalty from macro conditions right now — but the surface calm rests on a real economy z-score that is still negative at -0.672, reflecting a growth mix where [GDPNow's 4.3% Q2 tracking](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow) is being carried by AI capex and investment while consumer spending runs at a softer 1.6%. The cross-pillar tension is that markets are pricing the good news aggressively while the economy is still telling a bifurcated story, and [the Fed — holding at 3.50–3.75%](https://blog.kraken.com/economic-brief/may-20-2026) with headline CPI at 3.8% and a hot services print already on the books from April — cannot provide the easing impulse that would unambiguously validate the momentum surge. The most important near-term event for MRMI is Thursday's [April PCE release](https://www.bea.gov/data/personal-consumption-expenditures-price-index): consensus is tracking core around 0.30% MoM, but if the services acceleration that drove the April CPI beat flows through to PCE, the 6-month inflation delta begins moving meaningfully, the macro buffer starts to erode, and any simultaneous softening in real income or spending would hit both axes of stress at once. The path to LONG requires either the PCE to stay tame enough to keep the buffer near-intact while momentum holds, or the real economy z-score to turn positive on improving breadth data — and the [July 4 EU tariff deadline](https://ourtake.bakerbotts.com/post/102ms7t/trump-tariff-tracker-may-8-2026) looms as the event that could reverse the geopolitical de-escalation trade that is currently doing most of the heavy lifting on MMI.
