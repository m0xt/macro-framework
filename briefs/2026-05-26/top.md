MRMI is holding at +0.076 — squarely in CAUTION at 75% exposure — and the two pillars are pointing in the same general direction but not with enough conviction to push to full allocation. Markets absorbed the [Moody's Aaa→Aa1 downgrade](https://ratings.moodys.com/ratings-news/443154) without breaking: HY credit spreads are near multi-decade tights, the VIX closed at 16.74, and the rally has broadened well beyond mega-cap tech into small caps and equal-weight indices, which is the kind of participation that gives momentum durability rather than fragility. The macro side looks almost as benign on the surface — the stress score is at 0.82, the macro buffer is near its maximum tailwind, and hard data like April retail sales up 4.9% year-over-year and Atlanta Fed's Q2 GDP tracking at 4.3% are holding the real-economy read above water despite consumer sentiment hitting an [all-time low in the Michigan survey](https://www.spglobal.com/ratings/en/regulatory/article/consumer-pulse-us-consumers-resilience-could-be-tested-across-sectors-s101675447). The live risk is that the calm macro read is priced off data that largely predates the tariff pass-through: core PCE is already at 3.2% and forecasters are calling for headline PCE near [4.5% in Q2](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/15/inflation-rate-projected-to-hit-6percent-in-the-second-quarter-top-economic-forecasters.html) as costs importers have been absorbing start reaching shelf prices, and the Moody's downgrade is reinforcing upward pressure on real rates at the margin — a slow-burn tightening that doesn't show in spreads today but narrows the buffer. That split — easy financial conditions now, plausible inflation squeeze ahead — is precisely why MRMI sits where it does: positive momentum and a calm macro environment, but not enough margin of safety to go all-in. The posture moves to LONG if the next CPI and PCE prints stay benign and breadth holds; it moves toward CASH if tariff costs land visibly in the June inflation data while spending and jobs follow sentiment lower — either alone is manageable, but both together erode the buffer that is currently doing the heavy lifting.
