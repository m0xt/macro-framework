The market momentum gauge is at its highest reading of the year — MMI at +0.559 — and the signal is built on unusually solid ground across all three components. Financial conditions are doing the heaviest lifting: [HY credit spreads running at 274 basis points are near multi-year lows](https://www.macrotrends.net/3006/high-yield-spread), VIX is at 16.20 (well below its long-run average and down from above 30 in March), and the MOVE index at 73.33 confirms that bond markets have calmed — those three inputs together drove the FinCon component's +0.222 seven-day jump, the biggest swing in the dashboard this week. The [growth impulse notched its sharpest one-day gain on the back of an ADP payrolls beat](https://www.crestwoodadvisors.com/may-2026-economic-and-market-update/) — 122,000 private jobs in May versus 101,000 in April — while [copper holding above $6.40 a pound near record highs](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/21/copper-industrial-metals-inflation-bond-yields.html) keeps the commodity side of the growth impulse firmly positive. Breadth is the standout reading at 0.853, meaning stocks are getting genuinely wide support, but the [seven-day drift of -0.060 flags a real tension](https://cryptodaily.co.uk/2026/06/sp500-breadth-paradox-record-highs): the S&P ran nine straight weekly gains into the end of May and delivered a [historic 16% two-month return](https://www.crestwoodadvisors.com/may-2026-economic-and-market-update/), yet the advance-decline line — a raw count of stocks rising versus falling — went negative on several of those up days, a tell that semis, defense, and energy are increasingly carrying the index rather than the whole market participating. The breadth score is still high enough that this is a pattern to monitor rather than act on, but further concentration in leadership would be the first visible crack. The fastest path to a regime change runs through credit: at 274bp, HY spreads have almost no cushion, and any shock that pushes them materially wider would hit the FinCon component hard and fast enough to drag the MMI toward the threshold where the headline posture steps down from LONG.
