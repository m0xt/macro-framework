MRMI is sitting at +0.245 — just 0.005 below the +0.25 threshold that would flip the dashboard from CAUTION to LONG, meaning the framework is allocating at 75% exposure and effectively calling this a risk-on environment with one hand still on the exit. The market momentum side of the equation is doing the heavy lifting: with [high-yield spreads near multi-year tights at 274 basis points](https://streetstats.finance/rates/corporates), the VIX at 16.2, and a positive yield curve, financial conditions have snapped hard off May's bond-market stress and MMI has recovered to +0.559 — the corporate bond market in particular is pricing near-zero default concern, which is as clean a green signal as the framework gets. The economy is the softer half: [GDPNow has trimmed its Q2 estimate to 3.0%](https://www.atlantafed.org/cqer/research/gdpnow), [April payrolls came in at only 115K](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/empsit.nr0.htm) with unemployment at 4.3%, and consumers are drawing down savings to sustain spending — growth is visibly slowing even as [CPI runs at 3.8% with PPI at 6%](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/article/hot-cpi-report-likely-to-put-fed-on-guard-for-longer-lasting-inflation-meanwhile-rate-hike-odds-are-rising-141806600.html). What is keeping the macro buffer near its ceiling — 0.44 out of a maximum of 0.50 — is that inflation's *direction* has not yet turned: core CPI is running at 2.74% year-over-year with a flat six-month trend, so the framework is not penalizing the regime signal for price acceleration today, and stress remains calm at 1.3 despite rising. That is the fragile piece, because the [May ISM input-price index near 82](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/manufacturing-pmi-at-54-may-2026-ism-manufacturing-pmi-report-302786165.html) has historically led goods inflation by a few months, [new Chair Kevin Warsh](https://www.mpamag.com/us/mortgage-industry/industry-trends/key-dates-that-will-define-kevin-warshs-opening-months-as-fed-chair/576298) meets June 17 with markets pricing a one-in-three chance of a hike, and the immediate tests are Thursday's May jobs report — consensus already soft at 85–89K — and June 10 CPI: a payrolls miss combined with core re-accelerating above April's 2.8% would simultaneously pull the growth score lower and begin eroding the macro buffer, pushing MRMI away from LONG and deeper into CAUTION; a clean pass on both could finally tip it over the line.
