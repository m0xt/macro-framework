MMI moved from +0.25 — last week's knife-edge hold on the LONG/CAUTION line — to +0.59, settling firmly in the green as two of its three drivers surged together over the week. Financial conditions jumped +0.73 to +0.62, powered by VIX falling from 18.80 to 17.68 and bond volatility (MOVE) dropping sharply from 75.20 to 69.36; breadth ran +0.74 to +1.46 as the [U.S.-Iran ceasefire announced Sunday June 14](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/markets-rally-u-iran-peace-095021795.html) triggered a [sweeping Monday rally](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/live/stock-market-today-monday-june-15-224751171.html) — Dow to a fresh record, nearly every sector participating — exactly the kind of broad move that lifts both volatility and breadth readings at once. [May CPI](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/10/cpi-inflation-report-may-2026.html) came in at +4.2% year-over-year and initially fed the "rates held higher" narrative from last week's payrolls, but the market absorbed it with little damage: the headline was almost entirely energy-driven, core CPI printed a soft +0.2% month-over-month, and the ceasefire — by reopening the Strait of Hormuz and sending Brent crude down more than 5% — immediately undercut the supply shock behind that hot number. GII, the growth impulse index, is the lone drag: it fell -0.44 over the week to -0.31, with an additional -0.42 move on the day, suggesting that credit, copper, and rotation signals haven't yet caught up to the volatility compression — these tend to lag the headline equity moves and may take a few sessions to confirm. The outstanding risk is the [FOMC decision and dot plot on June 17](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm), new Chair Warsh's debut: the hold is essentially certain, but if the updated projections shift the 2026 median to zero cuts and the press conference signals a harder neutral stance, bond volatility would reprice fast and could reverse a meaningful chunk of the FinCon improvement that drove most of MMI's gain this week.
