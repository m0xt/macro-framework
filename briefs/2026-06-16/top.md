MRMI jumped from -0.06 to +0.25 this week — exactly at the LONG threshold, still in CAUTION at 75% exposure, but the move provides a direct answer to last week's central question: equities were more right than the bond market. [May CPI's](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_06102026.htm) soft core print (+0.2% month-over-month) defused the rate-repricing thesis, and the [U.S.-Iran ceasefire](https://finance.yahoo.com/markets/stocks/articles/markets-rally-u-iran-peace-095021795.html) then removed the energy supply shock driving the hot headline number, sending Brent crude down more than 5% and making the +4.2% year-over-year print look backward-looking almost immediately. That geopolitical relief did most of the work on the market side: MMI moved from +0.25 to +0.59 as financial conditions recovered +0.73 and breadth surged +0.74 on the broad rally — the kind of nearly universal sector participation that tells the model the move has width, not just noise. The economy pillar barely shifted — the macro buffer slipped from +0.44 to +0.41 and stress rose to +1.86, still firmly Calm — but what's underneath is more two-sided than before: growth is recovering (Real Economy Score from -0.98 to -0.46, GDPNow back to 3.3%) while the inflation direction axis warmed from +0.04 to +0.30, meaning the backdrop is stabilizing rather than cleanly improving. MRMI is parked at the exact number separating CAUTION from LONG, which puts the [FOMC decision and dot plot tomorrow](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm) — Chair Warsh's debut — in a live position: a balanced hold with unchanged projections lets the financial conditions recovery consolidate and gives the lagging growth impulse index time to catch up, which likely tips MRMI into LONG; a harder neutral signal reprices bond volatility fast, partially unwinds the MMI gain, and pushes MRMI back toward mid-CAUTION. Beyond tomorrow, the watch is the inflation direction reading: at +0.30 and drifting warmer, two more months of tariff pass-through showing up in core goods would start eroding the macro buffer from the inside — and that's the scenario where MRMI's path to a sustained LONG narrows even if equity markets stay calm.
