The stress score moved to +1.86 this week — up +0.68, but still firmly Calm — and the macro buffer feeding MRMI barely budged, slipping from +0.44 last week to +0.41. The previous brief flagged [May CPI](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_06102026.htm) as the key swing factor; it landed on June 10 and gave a split verdict. Core came in at +0.2% month-over-month, a small downside surprise against the +0.3% consensus, which is why core CPI YoY held at 2.90% and the buffer didn't crater. But the six-month directional change on core inflation is no longer near-flat: it accelerated from +0.04 pp to +0.30 pp, meaning inflation is slowly tilting warmer on the trajectory the framework watches most closely, and that's what pushed stress higher. On the other side of the ledger, the real-economy picture improved sharply: the Real Economy Score climbed from -0.98 to -0.46 (+0.53 on the week), and [Atlanta Fed GDPNow](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow) edged back up toward +3.3% annualized for Q2 — spending and investment holding up well enough to partially offset the soft-jobs-composition story from the prior brief. The offsetting moves — warming inflation direction, recovering growth — largely netted out, leaving the stress score modestly higher but the Calm reading intact and the MRMI buffer still near full strength. What would shift this next: the [FOMC decision and dot-plot update tomorrow (June 17)](https://www.federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm) could matter if Chair Warsh signals fewer cuts or a higher-for-longer bias, but the bigger test for the dashboard is whether the inflation direction axis keeps drifting positive over the next two CPI prints — if July and August data confirm that tariff pass-through is accelerating into core goods, the +0.30 reading could push toward the level where it starts meaningfully eroding the buffer and lifting stress toward the Watch band.
