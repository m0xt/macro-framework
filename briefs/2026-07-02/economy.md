The stress score crossed from Calm into Watch territory in a single week, rising to +3.83 (7d +1.94) as the growth side of the framework deteriorated sharply while inflation held still. The Real Economy Score fell to -0.98 (7d -0.52), driven by [GDPNow sliding from 2.5% to 1.2% annualized](https://investinglive.com/centralbank/atlanta-fed-gdpnow-tumbles-to-12-from-25-last-20260701/) after May's advance goods trade deficit widened by $22.7 billion to [$105.8 billion](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/us-trade-deficit-may-2026) — exports fell 5.4% as April's tariff-driven pull-forward surge reversed, while imports kept climbing on continued capital goods front-loading. That trade gap muddies the picture: [ISM Manufacturing expanded for a sixth consecutive month at 53.3%](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/manufacturing-pmi-at-53-3-june-2026-ism-manufacturing-pmi-report-302814991.html) and ISM Prices Paid fell 9.1 points to 73.0 — the sharpest monthly drop since July 2022 — suggesting tariff-driven input cost acceleration is easing even as headline prices stay elevated, so the GDP drag may be more trade-accounting distortion than actual demand destruction. [ADP's June private payroll count of 98,000](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/01/private-payrolls-rose-by-98000-in-june-less-than-expected-adp-reports.html) — below consensus and almost entirely services-driven — confirms the labor market is losing altitude, not just being hit by import math. Inflation Direction stayed flat at +0.30 (7d +0.00) with core CPI still at 2.90%, so the previous brief's watch-point — core CPI closing the gap to core PCE's 3.4% run rate — has not triggered; growth broke first. The macro buffer eroded to +0.31 from +0.40, still a positive tailwind to the headline allocation posture but with less cushion if growth keeps sliding. The June payrolls print, released this morning by the Bureau of Labor Statistics, is the immediate test: a result well below the [100,000 consensus](https://insight.factset.com/total-nonfarm-payrolls-for-june-2026-are-projected-to-rise-by-100000) or any uptick in unemployment above 4.3% would push the Real Economy Score deeper negative and move stress toward Building, while the [ISM Services report on July 6](https://www.ismworld.org/supply-management-news-and-reports/reports/rob-report-calendar/) and the [July 14 core CPI print](https://www.bls.gov/schedule/news_release/cpi.htm) determine whether this week's jump is a growth blip or the start of a sustained squeeze on the buffer.
