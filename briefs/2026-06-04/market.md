MMI moved up sharply today to +0.59, putting it well inside positive territory, but the seven-day read of -0.16 tells the more honest story of the week: momentum has been grinding lower, not higher. The culprit is FinCon, which fell -0.32 over seven days as the 30-year Treasury yield briefly crested 5% in late May, lifting the broader rate uncertainty that this component tracks. That episode is largely resolved — the 10-year has since pulled back around 14 basis points, and the MOVE sits at a contained 73.58, which allowed FinCon to recover +0.10 today. The standout reading right now is Breadth at +1.08, the strongest of the three MMI components, and it jumped another +0.32 today, signalling that [the equity rally is getting broader support](https://www.morningstar.com/markets/is-stock-market-rotation-underway-these-sectors-are-outpacing-tech-2026) rather than relying on a handful of names to hold the index up. GII also made a sharp one-day recovery of +0.28, consistent with credit spreads sitting near historical tights and an [ADP print of 122,000 private jobs in May](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/adp-jobs-report-may-2026-payrolls-increase-by-122000.html) that came in above expectations and was unusually broad-based across sectors. The pivotal number for the week ahead is Friday's [official May payrolls report](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-30/us-jobs-report-set-to-reveal-solid-growth-steady-unemployment-rate), where consensus is around 80,000: a materially stronger print would push yields back up, squeeze FinCon, and deepen the seven-day drag, while a soft-but-stable result would leave financial conditions loose enough for MMI to consolidate and build on today's recovery.
