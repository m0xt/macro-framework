The macro stress score sits at **1.2** — comfortably in the Calm zone — but it ticked up 0.4 points over the past week, which is worth watching even if the alarm is not yet ringing. The driver is almost entirely on the growth side: the Real Economy Score fell to –1.0, its weakest reading in recent weeks, as the weight of soft Q1 data keeps pulling the composite down. The [second estimate of Q1 GDP came in at 1.6%](https://www.bea.gov/news/2026/gdp-advance-estimate-1st-quarter-2026), revised down from 2.0%, with consumer spending contributing less than first reported, and two-thirds of households in recent surveys say they are cutting back due to higher prices. The Atlanta Fed's [GDPNow for Q2 was also trimmed to 3.0%](https://www.fx.co/en/forex-news/3007407) from 3.8% as of June 1, so while the level still looks decent, the direction of revision is the wrong one. Inflation, by contrast, is not adding to stress: core CPI is running at [2.74% year-over-year](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/archives/cpi_05122026.htm) and the six-month change in its trend is essentially flat at +0.04 percentage points, meaning the dashboard's two-headed stress amplifier — weak growth *and* rising inflation together — is not engaged. The week did produce genuine green shoots: [ADP private payrolls came in at 122,000 for May](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/03/adp-jobs-report-may-2026-payrolls-increase-by-122000.html), the best monthly gain since January 2025, with hiring broad-based across firm sizes, and the [ISM Manufacturing PMI jumped to 54.0](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/manufacturing-pmi-at-54-may-2026-ism-manufacturing-pmi-report-302786165.html) — a four-year high and the fifth straight month of factory expansion. The two readings that will materially move the stress score from here are tomorrow's May payrolls print (June 5) and the May CPI release (June 10): a weak jobs number would push the Real Economy Score further negative and lift stress toward the Watch zone, while a CPI surprise to the upside would engage the inflation axis and accelerate the erosion of the macro buffer currently providing nearly full tailwind to MRMI at +0.44.
