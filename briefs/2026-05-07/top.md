MRMI's +1.142 read is clean and unambiguous: the macro buffer is running at full strength precisely because the two inputs that would compress it — weakening growth and re-accelerating inflation — are not simultaneously present, and both the market and economy data are, unusually, telling the same story right now. Financial conditions confirm it: the [VIX at 17.55, MOVE at 70.63, and HY OAS at 277bp](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2) are all pricing near-zero systemic stress, and the macro data validates rather than contradicts that read — [core CPI has drifted down roughly 0.4 percentage points over six months](https://www.bls.gov/news.release/cpi.nr0.htm), which is exactly why the AND-gate stays closed and the buffer stays at 1.0. The single variable that could break this cross-pillar agreement is tariff pass-through: [JPMorgan estimates that businesses absorbed roughly 80% of tariff costs in 2025 but that ratio inverts through 2026](https://www.jpmorgan.com/insights/global-research/current-events/us-tariffs), meaning a consumer-side inflation impulse is in the pipeline even as the current CPI trend reads benign — the mechanism that would flip the inflation direction input before the real economy composite has time to recover. Growth is holding a floor for now via [Atlanta Fed GDPNow's Q2 read of 3.7%](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow), but strip that out and the underlying fundamental trio — PCE, Sahm, real income — is already sitting at −0.94σ, which means the real economy side of the AND-gate is closer to triggering than the headline number suggests. The joint policy flashpoint is [Kevin Warsh's first FOMC on June 16–17](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/live/fed-meeting-live-updates-federal-reserve-holds-interest-rates-steady-in-powells-final-meeting-as-chair-111906007.html): his rate posture will move both pillars simultaneously — a hawkish lean that strengthens the dollar and steepens financial conditions would pressure GII and erode the market momentum that is currently holding MMI at +0.642, while any softening in the inflation trajectory that gives him room to cut would keep the buffer intact. The signal is LONG and the framework's logic supports it, but the asymmetry is clear: this is a regime where the upside is gradual and the downside is event-driven, and the June FOMC is the first real test of whether that buffer earns its keep.
