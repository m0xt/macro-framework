The MMI sits comfortably green at +0.642, but the composition tells a more nuanced story — FinCon is doing the heavy lifting at the margin while GII remains the laggard, and Breadth's dominance at +1.136 is showing its first haircut of the week. The week's pivotal event was the [April 29 FOMC hold](https://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/pressreleases/monetary20260429a.htm) — the Fed kept rates at 3.5–3.75%, issued four dissents (the most since 1992), and maintained a one-cut median dot for 2026 — which resolved near-term policy uncertainty and gave financial conditions room to tighten further in a good way: the VIX has collapsed from its late-March spike to 31 all the way to 17.55, MOVE at 70.63 is running near post-tariff-chaos lows, and [HY OAS at 277bp](https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/BAMLH0A0HYM2) is as tight as it's been in the cycle, collectively driving FinCon's +0.154 weekly gain — the largest single-week improvement of the three sub-components. GII's more modest +0.311 reading is the honest friction in this picture: the yield curve offers only a 50bp 2s10s spread, and a [DXY printing 118](https://finance.yahoo.com/quote/DX-Y.NYB/) acts as a persistent headwind on the global growth impulse, capping the uplift that tight credit spreads and elevated copper prices would otherwise deliver. Breadth at +1.136 remains the strongest absolute contributor and reflects the genuine broadening of the earnings recovery — [blended S&P 500 EPS growth is running +27.1%](https://www.krilogy.com/weekly-market-recap-may-4-2026/), the best since 2021 — but this week's -0.107 slip is consistent with the narrow cyclical leadership reported for the May 4 week, where materials and industrials lagged while communication services and energy led. The key divergence to monitor: credit and volatility markets are pricing near-zero stress and giving the green light, but the global growth impulse won't fully confirm until the dollar softens or the yield curve steepens materially — and with [Kevin Warsh set to chair his first FOMC on June 16–17](https://finance.yahoo.com/economy/policy/live/fed-meeting-live-updates-federal-reserve-holds-interest-rates-steady-in-powells-final-meeting-as-chair-111906007.html), both the rate path and the dollar are open questions that keep GII tethered.
