Market momentum pulled back from its quarterly high of +0.70 to +0.51 (1d -0.20, 7d -0.26) this week, answering last week's open question bluntly: the breadth improvement was real but reactive to financials, and it could not survive a week of tech earnings disappointments. The largest single blow came Thursday when [Bloomberg reported Alphabet's Gemini 3.5 Pro flagship AI model is months behind schedule](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/16/alphabet-stock-gemini-3-5-pro-ai.html), sending Alphabet stock down 4.4% and erasing roughly $200 billion in market cap, while [TSMC's decision to lift its 2026 capital expenditure guidance to $60–$64 billion](https://finance.yahoo.com/technology/articles/tsmc-targets-40-sales-growth-194152888.html) — up from prior guidance of $52–$56 billion — sparked fresh debate about whether AI infrastructure spending will ever translate into proportionate revenue, pushing the Philadelphia Semiconductor Index 20% below its June record and into bear-market territory. Financial conditions bore the brunt: the component dropped to +0.40 (1d -0.18, 7d -0.50), the VIX jumped from 16.73 to 18.77, and the 7-day drag reflects anxiety that built steadily across the week rather than any single shock. Breadth fell to +1.05 (1d -0.30, 7d -0.24) — it held the 1.00 line, barely, but eight of eleven sectors still rose on the week, and the equal-weight index remains positive, so the rally isn't yet narrowing back to a handful of mega-cap names. Growth impulses slipped to +0.08 (1d -0.10, 7d -0.05) as [the Baltic Dry Index dropped to 2,752](https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/baltic-dry-index-falls-to-2752-down-88-points/), giving back a chunk of the gains that had accumulated over the prior fortnight, though the yield curve held its modest steepness with the 10-year at 4.57 and the 2-year at 4.16. [Alphabet reports Wednesday](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/17/stock-market-next-week-outlook-for-july-20-24-2026.html) alongside a heavy slate of industrials and consumer names — if Alphabet's capex guidance reinforces AI spending conviction and the broader earnings beat rate holds above 85%, breadth has a path to recover; if Alphabet disappoints again or capex commentary hardens the AI-ROI concern, the semiconductor rout could pull the rest of the market lower and push financial conditions below +0.30, which would be the clearest warning sign yet that this week's momentum pullback is the start of something more than a single-week digestion.
