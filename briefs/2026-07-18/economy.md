The macro stress score holds at +0.53 — Calm, with the macro buffer at +0.47, just below the full-strength ceiling — and the week's additional data confirmed rather than challenged the CPI-driven de-escalation. [June's producer price index dropped 0.3%](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/07/15/wholesale-inflation-june-2026-.html), led by a 12% gasoline crash pulling goods prices down 1.4%, which backed up the disinflation story and kept the inflation direction reading at +0.00; that said, wholesale service prices still ticked up 0.2% on the month and [Michigan's one-year-ahead inflation expectations stayed elevated at 4.2%](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/07/17/consumer-sentiment-hits-highest-level-since-february-on-easing-gas-prices), leaving last week's concern — whether June's calm was energy relief masking tariff pass-through in services — still unresolved. The growth picture looks better than the Real Economy Score's -0.71 suggests: [the Philadelphia Fed manufacturing index surged to 41.4 in July](https://www.rttnews.com/story.aspx?Id=3667688), nearly a five-year high against a 13.0 estimate, [the Empire State index recovered to 15.6](https://www.stateeconomicwatch.com/2026/07/solid-empire.html), and [Michigan consumer sentiment hit a five-month high of 54.4](https://www.advisorperspectives.com/dshort/updates/2026/07/17/consumer-sentiment-hits-highest-level-since-february-on-easing-gas-prices) on cheaper gasoline — all pointing to real-side activity that is holding up rather than deteriorating. The score improved only +0.11 on the week because the hard-data side has not yet caught up to the survey optimism: [retail sales grew just 0.2%](https://www.cnn.com/2026/07/16/economy/us-retail-sales-june), industrial production barely moved, and the June payrolls miss is still pulling the labor component of the composite below water. The two things that would shift the view are the same ones flagged last week: a July CPI print showing services costs accelerating on tariff pass-through, which would flip the inflation direction axis back above zero and rebuild stress fast; or the [July 30 advance Q2 GDP report](https://www.bea.gov/news/schedule) coming in well below [GDPNow's current tracking near 1.3%](https://www.atlantafed.org/research-and-data/data/gdpnow), deepening the growth deficit already holding the Real Economy Score in negative territory.
