The Problem

Macro indicators are everywhere. VIX, credit spreads, yield curves, breadth, liquidity, jobless claims, shipping indices...

The challenge isn't finding data. It's that most frameworks collapse it into a single answer — either telling you when to act, or where you are in the cycle. Almost never both.

Momentum signals tell you to act — but not whether the action has wind at its back.

Cycle readings tell you where you are — but not whether to do anything about it.

What we built:

  • Two pillars that confirm each other — a fast market signal and a slow economy buffer; the market alone never triggers cash
  • MMI — Market Momentum Index — three market signals at different speeds, equally-weighted into one composite
  • Macro Stress — a 0–1 score that fires only when growth weakens AND inflation rises (the stagflation pocket)
  • MRMI = MMI + macro buffer — the headline regime signal combining both pillars into one number
  • Single dashboard, four-step walkthrough — headline → market pillar → economy pillar → reference library

The Framework: Two Pillars, One Signal

Markets can flash false alarms — a single VIX spike, a credit blow-out, a sector rotation that reverses in days. The economy can't fake it: weak growth plus rising inflation is a real condition. We require both to agree before flipping to cash.

Pillar 1 — Market

What are markets signaling?

Built from price & volatility data — credit spreads, sector breadth, financial-conditions vol. Fast: catches turning points early. Trade-off: prone to false alarms.

Answered by → MMI — Market Momentum Index

Pillar 2 — Economy

Is the economy confirming?

Built from real-economy & inflation data — PCE, jobs, income, GDPNow, Core CPI direction. Slow: takes time to build. Strength: grounded in fundamentals, filters market noise.

Answered by → Macro Stress

The synthesis — MRMI: MRMI = MMI + (1 − Stress) − 0.5. The economy provides a default buffer that keeps us invested. Only when MMI turns red and Macro Stress builds (growth weak AND inflation rising) does the buffer erode and MRMI cross below zero. One number. One color. One action.

Pillar 1 — Market

MMI — Market Momentum Index

A daily composite that answers "What are markets signaling right now?" Three market-derived indicators, each capturing a different force at a different speed, normalized to a common scale and combined into one weighted signal.

IndicatorWhat It CapturesWindowSpeedWeight
Growth Impulses (GII) Momentum across credit, sectors, copper, vol, yield curve, shipping 21d ROC, 2yr z-score Fast ⅓
Sector Breadth Whether the rally is broad (cyclicals participating) or narrow 63d z-score Medium ⅓
Financial Conditions Equity vol + bond vol + credit spread = market stress 252d z-score Slow ⅓

What's a z-score? A z-score expresses today's value as standard deviations from its recent typical range. +2 = unusually high (above ~98% of recent observations). 0 = right at average. −2 = unusually low. This puts copper prices, VIX levels, and credit spreads — all on totally different scales — onto one comparable axis. The "window" column above is how far back we look to define "recent typical."

How the signal is produced

Each indicator outputs a z-scored composite. The equal-weighted average (1/1/1) becomes the MMI score. MMI feeds into MRMI = MMI + (1 − Stress) − 0.5 — the headline signal. MMI alone never triggers a CASH call.

Why three, not one

Three time horizons of market signaling: fast (positioning shifts), medium (rotation breadth), slow (structural stress). When fast indicators give false signals, the slow ones stabilize the composite — and vice versa.

Why These Three Indicators?

What We Tested

840+ parameter combinations per indicator. Grid search across lookback periods, smoothing, thresholds, component subsets. 70/30 in-sample / out-of-sample split.

What We Dropped

  • IG credit spread — adding it to Financial Conditions didn't improve the signal
  • SLX (Steel ETF) — noisy, hurt Sector Breadth performance
  • Baltic Dry Index — negative alpha out-of-sample across all assets
  • GII "both positive" mode — green only 5% of the time, too restrictive

What Survived

  • GII fast ROC (21d) — shorter than original (42d), catches turns faster
  • FinCon (252d) — longer than original (126d), more structural
  • Breadth (63d) — shorter than original (252d), more responsive
  • No smoothing — EMA consistently hurt performance across all indicators

The composite (MMI) outperforms every individual indicator because the three capture different things at different speeds. Breadth is the strongest standalone for equities (SPX +9.4%, IWM +19.3%) but is hit-or-miss on crypto. GII is the most balanced — modest on SPX (+6.0%) but the only one with consistently positive crypto alpha (+11.5%). FinCon adds structural SPX edge (+6.7%) but is actually negative on BTC (-8.2%), confirming it's an equity-specific signal.

Combining all three: MMI lifts SPX alpha to +12.6%, IWM to +20.3%, BTC to +13.4% — better than any individual on every asset. That's diversification of signals doing real work.

Pillar 2 — Economy

Macro Stress

A slow-moving economy-derived layer that answers "Is the economy confirming the market signal?" One score, in [0, 1], capturing how deep we are inside the stagflation pocket — the only macro condition that pulls MRMI toward CASH.

AxisWhat It MeasuresInputsReading
Real Economy Score Strength of real-economy activity PCE YoY · Sahm Rule (inv) · Real Income YoY · Atlanta Fed GDPNow, equal-weighted z-score over 3y Above 0 = healthy · Below 0 = weakening
Inflation Direction Trajectory of inflation, not its level Δ Core CPI YoY over the last 6 months, in pp Above 0 = accelerating · Below 0 = decelerating

The AND gate

Stress = min(1, max(0, −Real_Economy) × max(0, Inflation_Direction))

Each max(0, …) means the factor only contributes when it's adversely positioned: weak growth AND rising inflation. The product is non-zero only when both are. Stress fires (ON) when the score crosses 0.5.

Why an AND gate

Inflation alone or weakness alone is normal — neither warrants stepping out of risk assets on its own. Stagflation is the asymmetric scenario where the Fed can't ease and the economy is rolling. Requiring both filters out the false alarms market vol would otherwise produce.

Why it changes slowly

Real-economy data is reported monthly with publication lags. Macro Stress moves on weeks-to-quarters, not days. That's a feature: when the score is rising, it's a real condition, not noise. Fast pivots on macro data are almost always wrong.

Why a library, not more pillars

A simple two-question framework drives clean decisions. The library lives alongside — visible, interpretable, but not voting on the binary call. Add when useful, never to add.

When something gets promoted

If a library indicator repeatedly leads or contradicts the formal axes at meaningful turns — and adding it improves interpretive power without bloating the framework — it moves into MMI or Macro Stress.

The Key Finding

With same-day execution, MRMI's edge is robust even with 50bps friction (+6.0%).

But a 1-day delay collapses the alpha from +11.2% to roughly breakeven. A 5-day delay turns it negative.

Implication

MRMI flips ~9× per year and avg regime is ~30 days. Most of the alpha comes from catching the flip on the same bar — the buffer makes flips rarer, but when they happen, speed still matters.

In practice the framework's weekly Tuesday cadence works because cash-trigger events require both pillars to agree, and macro stress builds slowly. When MRMI does flip, same-week execution captures most of the available edge — but a multi-week delay materially erodes it.

What It Doesn't Do

Limitations

  • Not a crystal ball — it reacts to conditions, doesn't predict them. Lag is inherent.
  • Same-week execution required — when MRMI flips, capture it on the Tuesday meeting. A 1-day delay collapses SPX alpha from +11.2% to +1.0%; multi-week delays turn it negative.
  • Walk-forward tested 2017–2026 only — 10 years, all positive on SPX and IWM. But no data before 2016 means we can't test 2008 or dot-com era.
  • No position sizing — it's binary (in/out), not graduated. A more sophisticated approach would scale exposure by signal strength.
  • No asset allocation — tells you long vs cash, not which assets to favor within each regime.

What It Does Well

  • Drawdown protection — SPX max drawdown from -18.9% to -4.8% out-of-sample. This alone justifies the framework.
  • Works across equities — positive SPX and IWM alpha in 10/10 walk-forward years (2017–2026). BTC is more uneven but the average year is positive.
  • Simple decision — one number, one color, one action. No ambiguity.
  • Transparent — every component is visible in the scorecard. You can see exactly what's driving the signal.
  • Free data — no expensive subscriptions needed. Yahoo Finance + FRED.
