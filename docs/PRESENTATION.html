Pillar 2 · Economy

Macro Stress — the slow buffer

The question: "Is the economy confirming the market signal?" Built from two inputs that only matter together — the stagflation pocket. Reads on a 0 to 1 scale with three plain-language buckets.

OFF

< 0.05

The economy isn't in stress. The buffer protects against false market signals. Today.

BUILDING

0.05 — 0.30

One axis turning meaningfully adverse. Watch carefully; buffer is partially eroding.

ELEVATED

> 0.30

Both axes adverse. Buffer significantly compressed. Combined with weak MMI, MRMI flips to CASH.

Two inputs · the AND-gate

Stress only fires when growth is weak AND inflation is rising at the same time — the stagflation pocket. If either is benign, stress stays near zero regardless of how bad the other one is.

Stress = min(1, max(0, −Real_Economy) × max(0, Inflation_Direction))

Today: 0.03 → OFF. Growth is weak (real economy score −0.77) but inflation just barely flipped positive (+0.04pp Δ6m). The gate is now armed for the first time in months — but the inflation factor is still too small to multiply against weak growth into a meaningful product.

The AND-gate — today's math

Stress = max(0, −Real_Economy) × max(0, Inflation_Direction)

= max(0, +0.77) × max(0, +0.04) = 0.77 × 0.04 ≈ 0.03 → OFF

The April CPI print pushed inflation direction from −0.42pp last week to +0.04pp this week. The gate is now armed — but the inflation factor is still too small to multiply against weak growth into anything meaningful. One more month of hot CPI and stress climbs into BUILDING.

Why Core CPI Δ6m specifically

Core (excl. food + energy): headline is noisy on energy spikes; core captures persistent inflation.  ·  YoY: the print everyone watches (Fed, market, press).  ·  Δ6m: shorter than YoY-of-YoY (too laggy at 12m), longer than 3m (too noisy). Captures real turns in trajectory.
